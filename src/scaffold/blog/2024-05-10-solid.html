    SOLID

    2024/05/10
    Tags: SOLID

    Introduction

    SOLID comprises 5 principles to follow when in object-orientated designs.

    S - Single Responsibility

    This is often defined as: "Each software module should have one and only one reason to change."

    A class should encapsulate doing a specific task in a specific way. If a class tries to do too much it will end up coupling things together and become more difficult to use. When have multiple responsibilities it can be difficult to test one responsibility in isolation - tests, become longer, more complex, brittle and tied to implementation.

    Two terms around this that are helpful to understand what's going on are: cohesion and coupling.

    Class elements that belong together are cohesive. Classes with more than 1 responsibility tend to have less cohesion than classes with a single responsibility. Diagram. No relationship between elements in a class, should question why they are grouped together. relationships between classes is coupling (could be tight or loose depending on implementation). Tight coupling = Binds two (or more) details together in a way that's difficult to change.

    Strive for high cohesion and low coupling.

    Keep classes, small, focused and testable.
